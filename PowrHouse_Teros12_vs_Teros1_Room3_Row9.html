Key Findings
Critical

Teros 12 Reads Significantly Lower EC Than Co-Located Teros 1 Sensors

In Zone 9b where both sensor types are co-located, the Teros 12 consistently reads lower EC than the Teros 1 across all water content ranges. The Teros 1 uses complex dielectric permittivity (CDP), which separates the real and imaginary components of the dielectric signal — allowing it to independently measure both moisture and ionic conductivity. The Teros 12's simpler capacitance-based measurement cannot fully decouple these signals, particularly at low VWC where the pore water is more concentrated. The result is that the Teros 12 underreports EC, which matters if any zone steering or alert thresholds are based on Teros 12 EC readings.

Important

Teros 12 VWC Bottoms Out Higher — Misses Deep Drybacks

The Teros 12 in Zone 9b has a minimum VWC floor of 12.2% vs the co-located Teros 1's minimum of 11.2%. Kevin's observation — "12 definitely can't read at low WC" — is confirmed: as the substrate dries below ~20%, the Teros 12's capacitance measurement loses sensitivity and the reading stagnates. This means AROYA and AutoMait may be calculating shallower drybacks than actually occurring when relying on Teros 12 data, potentially irrigating later than the true moisture state warrants.

Context

Why: Capacitance vs Complex Dielectric Permittivity

The Teros 1 uses complex dielectric permittivity (CDP), measuring both the real part (energy storage, dominated by water content) and the imaginary part (energy loss, dominated by ionic conductivity/EC). This allows it to independently resolve VWC and EC even at low moisture levels. The Teros 12 uses a simpler single-frequency capacitance approach — it measures total impedance without separating the resistive and reactive components. At low WC, the pore water EC rises (same ions, less water), which bleeds into the capacitance reading and skews both the VWC and EC outputs simultaneously.

Recommendation

Use Teros 1 Readings as Primary for AutoMait Steering in Row 9

For AutoMait crop steering decisions in both Zone 9 and Zone 9b, the Teros 1 sensors should be the primary controlling sensors. In Zone 9b specifically, ensure AutoMait is weighted toward or exclusively using the Teros 1 devices (H3440016194, H3440016197, H3440016194-2) rather than the Teros 12 units (H3410003364, H3410003365, H3410003355). The Teros 12s can stay deployed for comparison visibility but should not drive irrigation timing, dryback thresholds, or EC-based alerts.
