Today's topic — irrigation for 3-gal coco, 14–15d veg, dryback P2→P3

3-gal coco with a 14–15 day veg puts you at a very specific size/volume ratio going into flip: a moderately-sized plant that can drink but has lots of substrate to carry. That changes what P2 and P3 should look like compared with the smaller-pot, shorter-veg programs most crop-steering recipes are tuned against.

How P2 and P3 should behave in a 3-gal coco block

P2 — maintenance irrigation (daylight middle)
  • Shots are maintenance-only: replace what the plant drank, keep VWC inside a ~5% band.
  • Event size 2–4% of substrate volume; frequency driven by drinking rate, not a fixed clock.
  • In 3-gal coco, plants take longer to re-saturate between events than a 1-gal — fewer, slightly larger P2 shots usually win over more, smaller ones.
  • Target zero runoff in P2 — P2 runoff means you're already into P1 territory.
  • On the graph: flat-ish VWC line with small sawtooth, pore EC stable.
P3 — pre-overnight dryback window
  • Last P2 of the day ends 4–6 hours before lights-off (3-gal is the long end of this — it dries slower).
  • Dryback target set by phase:
    · Early flower (wk 1–2): 15–20%
    · Mid bulking (wk 3–5): 20–30%
    · Late flower / ripening: 25–40%
  • Dryback is measured as the graph drops — a programmed P3 that never lands below 45% isn't a dryback, it's a declaration.
  • Pore EC should rise during P3 (water leaving, salts staying). If EC doesn't climb overnight, the plant isn't drinking — substrate temp, root zone pH, or plant stress is the first place to look.

What to watch in the first 72 hours after any program change

  • Dryback floor. Does VWC drop below 35% before first P1? If no, extend P3 by 60-minute steps until it does.
  • Overnight EC slope. Rising ~0.2–0.5 per night is healthy in a bulking phase. Flat = not drinking. Jumping > 1.0 = either a recipe change landed or the plant is in stress.
  • Field capacity after P1. First shot of the morning should restore to the previous morning's high, not overshoot it. Chasing field capacity upward day over day means you're over-irrigating.
  • Substrate temp. Every 5°F above 75°F makes this whole feedback loop fuzzier. In F4 specifically, don't change irrigation until the temp is back under 77°F — you'll be tuning against a broken signal.
